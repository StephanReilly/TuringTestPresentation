The Turing Test
A presentation by Stephan N. Reilly :)

The Turing Test was developed by Alan Turing in 1950.
The purpose of The Turing Test is to determine if a machine can exhibit behaviour equal to or indistibguishable from a human.
Here's a short video explaining how it works.

Many thought we would never reach a point where machines outsmarted humans but in 1997 IBM's "Big Blue"
defeated the world chess grandmaster marking the first time in history
a computer beat humanity's best at their own game.

IBM's "Big Blue" passed the Turing Test by displaying behaviour indistinguishable from a human's making it one of the most historic computers ever built.
